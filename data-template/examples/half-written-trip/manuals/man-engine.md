## Raw-water cooling {#raw-water}

Sea water is drawn through the seacock and strainer, pushed by the engine-driven
raw-water pump through the heat exchanger, then discharged with the exhaust. A
healthy engine throws a steady stream of water at the exhaust outlet. **Weak or
no flow at the exhaust, with a rising temperature, points first at the raw-water
side**: a closed seacock, a clogged strainer, an airlock, or — most commonly — a
worn or shredded impeller in the raw-water pump.

## Impeller service {#impeller}

The raw-water pump uses a flexible neoprene impeller that wears and eventually
loses vanes; a missing vane both cuts flow and can lodge downstream in the heat
exchanger. Inspect annually and carry a spare.

To replace:

- [ ] Close the raw-water seacock before opening the pump.
- [ ] Remove the pump cover plate and gasket.
- [ ] Draw the old impeller straight off the shaft; count the vanes and account
      for any that broke off (retrieve them from the heat exchanger if missing).
- [ ] Smear the new impeller with a little glycerine, twist it on following the
      curl of the vanes, fit a new cover gasket.
- [ ] Re-open the seacock, start the engine, and confirm a strong stream at the
      exhaust before getting under way.
