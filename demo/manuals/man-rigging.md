Standing-rigging reference for *Valkyrie*, a masthead-sloop Catalina 25. The wire
is **1/4 in 1x19 stainless** throughout, with swaged terminals at the upper ends
and turnbuckles at the chainplates.

The standing rigging comprises:

- Single upper shroud, each side (masthead to outboard chainplate)
- Single lower shroud, each side (spreader base to inboard chainplate)
- Forestay, masthead to stemhead fitting
- Backstay, masthead to transom chainplate

## Mast stepping {#stepping}

Step the mast at the tabernacle with the boom and a controlled gin-pole or crane.
Pin the forestay and backstay first to capture the masthead fore-and-aft, then
the uppers, then the lowers. Leave all turnbuckles slack until the rig is plumb.

## Tuning the rig {#tuning}

Set the shrouds hand-tight at the dock and the mast plumb athwartships. Then sail
the boat and adjust the leeward shrouds to just-slack on the wind — when the
leeward uppers go soft, take up until they hold without going bar-tight. Tack and
repeat both ways so the mast stays in column on either tack. The annual loft
inspection (m-standing-rigging-inspect) re-tunes to spec.

## Wire + terminal specs {#wire}

1/4 in 1x19 316 stainless wire; swaged eye/stud terminals aloft; bronze or
stainless open-body turnbuckles at the chainplates with cotter pins, not rings.
Replace wire and swages on a ~10-year cycle for coastal use, sooner if any
cracking shows.

## Inspection checklist {#inspection}

- [ ] Swages + terminals for cracks, rust weep, or bent strands
- [ ] Chainplates for crazing and elongated pin holes
- [ ] Cotter/clevis pins present, spread, and taped
- [ ] Turnbuckle threads cleaned and greased
