Spotted on the shakedown sail: several broken strands just below the shackle
splice, right where the halyard turns over the masthead sheave under load. The
core was starting to show, so this went straight to the top of the list rather
than waiting for the next haulout.

Measured the old halyard end-to-end at roughly 60 ft. Took it to the loft, who
matched the line and re-spliced the original snap shackle onto the new halyard.
Re-rove cleanly and the jib hoists smoothly again with no chafe at the sheave.
