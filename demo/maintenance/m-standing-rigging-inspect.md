Pre-season standing-rigging check by the loft before stepping back into the
sailing season: swages and terminals, turnbuckles, cotter and clevis pins, and
the chainplates where they pass through the deck. On a wire-rigged boat this is
the one inspection worth paying for every year.

Everything came back sound. One clevis pin showed wear and was renewed, and the
turnbuckles were backed off, greased, re-tensioned, and re-pinned. No crazing at
the chainplates and no broken strands at any swage. Good for the season.

## Steps
- [x] Inspect swages + terminals.
- [x] Check chainplates for crazing.
- [x] Renew worn clevis pin.
- [x] Grease + re-pin turnbuckles.
