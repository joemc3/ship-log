Poor VHF range was noticeable on the delivery — couldn't raise the marina until
we were almost in the approach, and a handheld outperformed the fixed set on the
same channels. That usually means a corroded masthead connector or water-logged
coax, not the radio.

Not urgent enough to send a rigger up just for this, so deferring to the next
mast-down (or pairing it with other aloft work). Replacing the antenna and the
full coax run end-to-end is the reliable fix rather than chasing one connector.

## Steps
- [ ] Bench-test radio + handheld to isolate.
- [ ] Order antenna + RG-8X coax.
- [ ] Replace at masthead (rigger aloft).
- [ ] Range-test on the water.
