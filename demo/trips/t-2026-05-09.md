The longest passage of the year — a delivery run down the coast from Gull Point
to Port Almira, just Miguel and me. We slipped Gull Point at dawn in light fog,
feeling our way out on the GPS and radar until Cape Verde Light came up out of
the murk and the fog finally burned off around nine.

The morning was dead calm, so we motor-sailed the flat water. That's where the
scare came: passing Halfway Shoal under sustained power, the engine temperature
crept up further than I liked. Raw-water flow at the exhaust looked weak, so I
throttled back and we sailed the rest on the building SW breeze rather than push
it. Classic impeller symptoms — logged it high priority.

By afternoon the wind had filled to a proper 15 kt and we had a fine reach down
to Port Almira, tying up at the transient dock by 15:00. One other gripe: the
masthead VHF range was poor the whole trip — couldn't raise stations I should
have heard easily, so the antenna or the coax run is suspect. Logged that too.
