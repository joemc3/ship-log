First proper sail of the 2024 season. We motored out of the fairway into a
glassy morning, but by midday the SW sea breeze had filled in and she came
alive on a reach across to Gull Point — heeled to the rail and tracking
beautifully.

Anchored in 9 ft of sand off the point in 5:1 scope and the hook bit on the
first try. The trouble showed up on the way out: while dousing the jib I
spotted the halyard badly frayed just below the head shackle — enough strands
gone that I'm surprised it held. Logged it as a high-priority squawk; that
halyard does not go up again until it's replaced.

Otherwise a flawless shakedown. Home on the building breeze, tied up by 15:45.
