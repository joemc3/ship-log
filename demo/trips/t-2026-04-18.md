First sail after the winter layup — a single-handed systems shakedown to make
sure nothing came apart over the cold months. Motored out under an overcast sky
that cleared as the morning went on, then shook out the sails in the inner bay
and worked methodically through every system: rig tension, sheets and halyards,
engine, electronics, through-hulls.

She's basically sound. Two squawks worth logging, though. The galley sink
seacock is stiff to operate and weeping slightly at the gland packing — that
gets serviced before any summer guests are aboard. And the bow nav light is
intermittent; it flickers whenever the bow pulpit takes a load, so I suspect a
chafed or corroded connection up forward.

Quiet, useful day. Tied up by mid-afternoon with a short punch-list to start
the season.
