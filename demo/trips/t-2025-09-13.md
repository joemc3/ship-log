First overnight of the season, and a fine one. We slipped the dock just after
lunch and carried the last of the flood through The Narrows, then close-reached
up the coast in a steady NW breeze. Anchored at Heron Island in 12 ft of sand,
5:1 scope, and she set hard when we backed down on her.

A quiet night at anchor — full moon over the island, dinner aboard in the
cockpit, and the breeze easing to nothing by midnight. Holding was excellent;
nobody touched the anchor watch alarm once.

Easy sail home the next afternoon on a close reach. The one thing worth logging:
while flaking the rode I noticed some chafe at the 30 ft splice. Nothing urgent,
but I want it inspected before the next overnight — noted against the ground
tackle in inventory.
