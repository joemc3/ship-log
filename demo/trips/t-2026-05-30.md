An easy club social sail with guests aboard — Dana and the Patels — on a picture
of a sea-breeze day. Bright sky, scattered cumulus, and a steady southerly that
gave us a long broad reach down to Gull Point. We hove-to off the point for
lunch in the cockpit, then reached back home in the afternoon.

The Patels' first time sailing and they loved it; nobody got seasick and
everyone took a turn at the helm. The only thing to log was at the dock: the
bilge pump's automatic float switch was cycling erratically — kicking on and off
when it shouldn't. The pump itself runs fine on the manual switch, so it's the
float switch that wants attention, not the pump. Squawked it and called it a day.
